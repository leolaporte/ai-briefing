Even more good news for the future of neurosymbolic AI

Date: 15-Apr-2026 2:55PM

"On the 3-block task, the neuro-symbolic model achieves 95% success compared to 34% for the best-performing VLA. The neuro-symbolic model also generalizes to an unseen 4-block variant (78% success), whereas both VLAs fail to complete the task." -- Timothy Duggan, Pierrick Lorang, Hong Lu, and Matthias Scheutz

Gary Marcus argues that a new Tufts research paper vindicates Apple's 2025 reasoning paper by demonstrating that neurosymbolic AI models significantly outperform large language models and vision-language-action models on generalization and reasoning tasks.

The Tufts paper extends Apple's findings showing that LLMs fail to generalize beyond their training data, specifically on the Tower of Hanoi problem. By combining neural networks for pattern recognition with symbolic planning, the neurosymbolic hybrid achieved 95% success on complex tasks where pure neural models only reached 34%, while also operating with nearly two orders of magnitude better energy efficiency. This research supports Marcus's long-standing argument that the future of AI should prioritize hybrid neurosymbolic approaches over pure LLM development, as different tools are needed for different jobs in artificial intelligence.
